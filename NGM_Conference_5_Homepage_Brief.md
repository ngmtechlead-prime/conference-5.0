# NGM Conference 5.0 — Homepage Design Brief

## Project Context

This is the homepage for **NGM Conference 5.0**, the flagship annual conference of the Nasir Giwa Mentorship (NGM) Platform. This year's edition marks NGM's **10th Anniversary**. The conference theme is **"Building the Future: The Urgency of Now"** and is scheduled for **October 3, 2026**.

### Primary Goals
1. Sell tickets to individual attendees
2. Attract business sponsors via a "Partner With Us" section

### Primary Audience
- University students
- Early-stage career professionals

| Student | ₦10,000 | ₦7,500 |

## Design Philosophy

**Editorial meets cinematic scroll storytelling.**

The page should feel like a sequence of scenes, not a stack of content blocks. Each section is a distinct visual moment. As the user scrolls, the page reveals information in a way that mirrors the emotional arc:

**Curiosity → Excitement → Credibility → Urgency**

### Core Aesthetic Principles
- **Typography as the hero element** — words do the visual heavy lifting throughout. Display type should be bold, oversized, and confident.
- **Grid-breaking layouts** — avoid uniform, predictable section structures. Use asymmetry, overlap, and scale contrast intentionally.
- **Scene changes** — each section should feel visually distinct from the one above it. Contrast in density, scale, and rhythm keeps the scroll engaging.
- **Editorial restraint where needed** — not every section should be loud. Contrast between high-energy and calm sections makes both more effective.

### Typography Direction
- Pair a strong, distinctive display font with a clean, readable body font
- Avoid generic font choices (Inter, Roboto, Arial, Space Grotesk)
- Display type should feel confident and contemporary — suited to a high-energy conference for young professionals

### Motion & Animation
- Page load: staggered reveal of hero elements
- Scroll-triggered animations for number counters in the Momentum section
- Hover states on competition cards and speaker cards
- Countdown timer on the Tickets CTA section

## Section Structure & Layout Direction

### 1. Hero
**Job:** Hook immediately. Communicate the conference identity, date, and 10th anniversary in one powerful visual moment.

**Layout:**
- Full viewport height
- Massive display typography dominating the screen — conference name and theme should feel like a cinematic title card
- 10th Anniversary callout prominently placed
- Conference date (October 3, 2026) clearly visible
- Single primary CTA button — "Get Your Ticket"
- Countdown timer visible in the hero or just below it

**Aesthetic:** Dark, high contrast, bold. This sets the tone for the entire page.

---

### 2. What is NGM Conference
**Job:** Briefly orient first-time visitors. Answer "what is this?" before asking them to feel anything about it.

**Layout:**
- Intentional contrast from the hero — open, generous white space
- Smaller, more considered typography — this is the one moment on the page where the user slows down to read
- One strong anchoring visual or graphic element on one side
- Copy should be concise — strictly about the conference, not the broader NGM community
- Key facts to communicate: what the conference is, who it's for, why it exists

**Aesthetic:** Clean and calm. Visual relief after the hero's intensity.

---

### 3. Momentum / Trajectory Section
**Job:** Communicate growth and scale. Create FOMO — this is the biggest edition yet and people don't want to miss it.

**Layout:**
- Break the grid here — oversized numbers that almost overflow their containers
- Tell the attendee growth story visually and horizontally:
  - Conference 1.0 → 700+ attendees
  - Conference 2.0 → 1,000+ attendees
  - Conference 3.0 → 1,500+ attendees
  - Conference 4.0 → 1,500+ attendees
  - Conference 5.0 → 2,000 target
- Each number should feel physically larger or more prominent than the last — the design should feel like it's building momentum
- Scroll-triggered number counters recommended
- Framing: this is about **trajectory and momentum**, not a retrospective. The story is "look where this is going" not "look what we've done"

**Aesthetic:** Kinetic, almost brutalist in typographic scale. High energy.

---

### 4. The Competitions
**Job:** Showcase the most unique and differentiating element of the conference. Two flagship competitions with significant prize value.

**Competitions to feature:**
1. **NGM x Kola Adesina DARE Nigeria Challenge** — A flagship innovation competition co-anchored with Dr. Kola Adesina, tied to a ₦30,000,000 pledge made at Conference 4.0, structured over three years of national impact.
2. **NGM x Afropreneur SME Pitch 5.0** — Entrepreneurship grant programme backed by a ₦100,000,000 multi-year pledge from Afropreneur, in partnership with Verraki Partners. Designed to create real economic impact for early-stage entrepreneurs in Nigeria.

**Layout:**
- Full-width split screen — left half for DARE Nigeria Challenge, right half for SME Pitch
- Each side has its own strong visual treatment within the section
- Hover interaction: hovered side expands slightly to draw focus
- Each competition card should communicate: name, brief description, prize/grant value, and a CTA

**Aesthetic:** Bold, distinct. The two competitions are different enough to each feel unique side by side.

**Important note:** The detailed agenda is not yet finalized. Present the high-level programme structure for now. The section should be designed so that detailed agenda items can be populated into it later without a redesign.

**High-level programme structure to display:**
1. Medical Session
2. Pre-event Session
3. Breakfast Session
4. Keynote Speech and Panel Session
5. Competitions and Awards

**Layout:**

- Clean, structured, and scannable
- Calm rhythm — this section is a deliberate pace change after the energy of the competitions section

**Aesthetic:** Structured and rhythmic. Ordered and clear.

## What to Avoid
- Generic layouts — uniform grids, predictable card patterns
- Overused font families (Inter, Roboto, Arial, Space Grotesk)
- Sections that feel visually identical to each other
- Presenting community-level NGM impact stats (e.g. scholarship rates, career advancement percentages) — this website is strictly about the conference
- Placeholder lorem ipsum content — use the actual copy provided in this brief
- A timeline of NGM's history — this is handled by the Momentum section
