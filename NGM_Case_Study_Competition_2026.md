## Competition Overview

The **Nasir Giwa Mentorship (NGM) Case Study & Research Analysis Competition** is a structured, multi-stage intellectual challenge designed to develop analytical thinking, research capacity, and problem-solving skills among participants. This competition provides a platform for emerging talents to engage with real-world business scenarios, evaluate economic dynamics, and propose innovative, data-driven solutions.

The year's competition will be conducted in **four (4) progressive stages**, each designed to test distinct competencies ranging from written communication to strategic analysis and global economic awareness.

### Objectives

1. To promote understanding of both local and global economic systems
2. To cultivate critical thinking and structured problem-solving skills
3. To bridge theoretical knowledge with real-world business challenges
4. To identify and nurture high-potential talents within and outside the NGM network
5. To enhance participants' research and analytical capabilities

---

## Competition Structure

### Application Stage — Case Study Submission
Participants will submit a well-structured analysis of a mini case study on a specified topic aligned with business or economic development.

### Stage 1 — Shortlisting
The best **20 individuals** are shortlisted and grouped into **10 groups of 2 members** each.

### Stage 2 — Local Case Study Analysis (Nigeria-Based Company)
The 10 groups will analyse a Nigerian company (product or service-based), evaluating its business model, market positioning, challenges, and growth opportunities. Only **7 teams** will proceed to the next stage.

### Stage 3 — Global Case Study & Research Challenge
The 7 groups will analyse an international company, focusing on global strategy, innovation, and adaptability in a competitive market. **5 teams** will proceed to the final stage.

### Final Stage — Global Impact Analysis
Finalists will present a comprehensive analysis of how major global disruptions (such as epidemics, global conflicts, or economic depressions) affect the Nigerian local economy.

## Judging Criteria

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Analytical Thinking** | 25% | Logical breakdown of the problem; identify root causes and connect disparate data points into a cohesive argument. |
| **Research Quality** | 20% | Use of credible, high-quality sources (primary and secondary); all claims backed by rigorous, accurate, and comprehensive evidence. |
| **Originality & Innovation** | 15% | Ability to move beyond standard solutions; introduce a unique perspective, new framework, or creative approach. |
| **Clarity & Communication** | 15% | Ideas presented in a structured, easy-to-follow manner so the judging panel and attendees can quickly grasp key insights. |
| **Practical Application** | 15% | Recommendations must be "real-world ready" — feasible, cost-effective, with a clear implementation roadmap and measurable outcomes. |
| **Presentation & Delivery** | 10% | High visual standards with professional formatting; impactful charts or graphs; findings delivered with confidence and professionalism. |

## Key Stakeholders and Responsibilities

### NGM HQ
- Provides the vision and final approval for all competition activities
- Manages the NGM identity and secures necessary resources or sponsorships
- Provides mentorship and overall guidance for the competition

### NGM Conference Team
- Agrees on structure with the NGM Case Study & Research Analysis Team (judges, topics, timeline, and participants)
- Drives publicity and competition awareness
- Monitors competition progress and participant reports
- Follows up on implementation and feedback from NGM HQ

- Agrees on structure with the NGM Conference Team (judges, topics, timeline, and participants)
- Drives publicity and competition awareness
- Provides the tools, training, templates, or background reading materials for the competition

- Follows up on implementation and feedback from NGM HQ

### Analysts / Competitors
- Attend all trainings and preparatory meetings as scheduled
- Conduct thorough research and analysis for each stage of the competition
- Develop and deliver high-quality presentations within given timelines
- Collaborate effectively and adhere to all competition guidelines and deadlines
